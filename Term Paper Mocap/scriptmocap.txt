lets start with the basics

Motion capture commonly known as mocap, is a technology that 
records motions of objects or people digitally. 
Cameras and sensors monitor the movement of particular 
body part and record their positions in space as time passes. 
then Digital figures are animated using this 
data to move in a realistic way.


mocap technology has evolved over centuries through 
advancements in spatial measuring,
 photography, and computing, 
leading to its wide application in fields such as animation, 
biomechanics,and entertainment, 

lets move on to its types

mocap systems are categorized into optical and non optical systems
under optical systems there are 3 types passive a ctive and markerless
• Passive Marker Systems: Use reflective markers; 
advantages include high frame rates and no cables required
• Active Marker Systems: Use LEDs; advantages
 include high signal-to-noise ratio and minimal marker jitter
• Markerless Systems: Example - Universal Capture used in The 
Matrix films for photorealistic results

now non optical system consists of inertial,mechanical,stretch sensors
and magnetic systems
• Inertial Systems: Use inertial sensors; advantages 
include portability and large capture areas
• Mechanical Systems: Use exoskeletons; advantages 
include real-time data and no occlusion
• Stretch Sensors: Flexible and resistant to
 magnetic interference but have higher latency
• Magnetic Systems: Use magnetic fields; 
advantages include real-time monitoring but are 
vulnerable to interference

 of actors, which were then transferred 
to the computer-generated characters. 
This allowed for incredibly realistic and expressive
 alien characters, blending seamlessly 
with live-action elements and creating a visually stunning 
and immersive cinematic experience. 
The success of "Avatar" showcased the potential of mocap in filmmaking, pushing the boundaries of 
visual effects and character animation.
